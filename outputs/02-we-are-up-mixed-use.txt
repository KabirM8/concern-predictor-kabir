### Ranked List of Likely Public Concerns

1. **Biological Resources**
   - **Concern:** Filling of existing wetlands.
   - **Rationale:** The project proposes to fill 13,470 square feet of wetlands, which could disrupt local ecosystems and wildlife habitats. Public concern may arise regarding the adequacy of the proposed mitigation measures and the potential long-term impacts on biodiversity.

2. **Hydrology and Water Quality**
   - **Concern:** Impact on water quality due to wetland conversion.
   - **Rationale:** The conversion of upland areas into new wetlands raises concerns about how this change will affect local hydrology and water quality. Residents may worry about runoff, sedimentation, and the overall health of nearby water bodies.

3. **Land Use and Planning**
   - **Concern:** Compatibility of mixed-use development with surrounding areas.
   - **Rationale:** The introduction of a mixed-use community center and residential units may raise concerns about how this development fits within the existing land use patterns of McKinleyville. Residents may be apprehensive about increased density and potential changes to neighborhood character.

4. **Transportation**
   - **Concern:** Increased traffic and accessibility issues.
   - **Rationale:** The addition of 70 residential units and a community center is likely to increase traffic in the area. Public concerns may focus on whether existing roads can accommodate this increase and how it will affect pedestrian and vehicle safety.

5. **Utilities and Service Systems**
   - **Concern:** Adequacy of water and sewer services.
   - **Rationale:** Connecting to the McKinleyville Community Services District for water and sewer raises questions about the capacity of these systems to handle the additional demand from the new development. Residents may be concerned about potential service disruptions or inadequacies.

6. **Population and Housing**
   - **Concern:** Impact on housing availability and affordability.
   - **Rationale:** While the project aims to provide housing for seniors and individuals with disabilities, there may be concerns about whether this development addresses broader housing needs in the community, particularly in terms of affordability and availability for other demographics.
